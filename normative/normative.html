Principles and Values of MLKN.lab

MLKN.lab is built on a foundation of principles and values that guide our research, design, and vision for the future of knowledge. These principles are not just abstract ideals—they are actionable frameworks that shape how we structure, share, and apply knowledge.

Polyhierarchy

Knowledge is not linear or flat—it’s multidimensional and interconnected. MLKN.lab’s polyhierarchical model allows us to represent knowledge as a dynamic, layered network, where disciplines, concepts, and ideas are connected in complex ways.

Connected to:
  • Research Focus: Computational epistemology, network science.
  • Normative Layer: Systems thinking, complexity.
  • Societal Dream: A world where knowledge is connected and accessible.

Open Science

Knowledge should be accessible, transparent, and reproducible. MLKN.lab is committed to open access, ensuring that our resources are freely available to everyone, everywhere.

Connected to:
  • Research Focus: Open Access Monographs, Scientific References.
  • Normative Layer: Transparency, collaboration.
  • Societal Dream: A world where knowledge is a public good.

Interdisciplinarity

The most pressing challenges of our time—from climate change to AI ethics—require interdisciplinary solutions. MLKN.lab breaks down silos between disciplines, fostering connections between AI, education, human rights, and more.

Connected to:
  • Research Focus: Multilayer networks, hypergraphs.
  • Normative Layer: Collaboration, diversity.
  • Societal Dream: A world where disciplines work together.

Ethical Knowledge

Knowledge is not neutral—it has ethical implications. MLKN.lab integrates ethical frameworks into our knowledge network, ensuring that our work aligns with human rights, fairness, and responsibility.

Connected to:
  • Research Focus: Human-AI Interaction, Cognitive Architectures.
  • Normative Layer: Ethics, human rights.
  • Societal Dream: A world where knowledge is used for good.

Societal Impact

Knowledge should not exist in a vacuum—it should serve society. MLKN.lab is designed to have a real-world impact, from informing policy to inspiring education to fostering innovation.

Connected to:
  • Research Focus: Knowledge Infrastructure, Applications.
  • Normative Layer: Justice, equity, sustainability.
  • Societal Dream: A world where knowledge transforms lives.

Societal Dreams: Building a Better Future

MLKN.lab is not just a research project—it’s a contribution to a broader movement to build a more connected, ethical, and knowledgeable society. Below are some of the societal dreams that inspire our work, along with how MLKN.lab is helping to turn these dreams into reality.

A Connected Society

Dream: A world where knowledge is interconnected, and disciplines, cultures, and ideas are no longer siloed.

How MLKN.lab Contributes:
  • Polyhierarchical Model: Represents knowledge as a dynamic network.
  • Interdisciplinary Focus: Breaks down silos between AI, education, human rights.
  • Open Access: Makes knowledge accessible to all.
Key Publications:
  • Boccaletti, S., et al. (2014). The Structure and Dynamics of Multilayer Networks
  • Nersessian, N. J. (2022). Interdisciplinarity in the Making

Ethical AI

Dream: A world where AI systems are designed and used ethically, aligning with human rights, fairness, and transparency.

How MLKN.lab Contributes:
  • Normative Knowledge Framework: Provides a framework for ethical AI.
  • Human-AI Interaction Focus: Explores how humans and AI can collaborate responsibly.
  • Ethics Layer: Integrates ethical principles into our knowledge network.
Key Publications:
  • Gunkel, D. (2023). Person, Thing, Robot
  • Council of Europe. (2023). Ethical Principles for AI

Knowledge for All

Dream: A world where knowledge is a public good, freely accessible to everyone, everywhere.

How MLKN.lab Contributes:
  • Open Access Monographs: A curated library of open-access resources.
  • Scientific References: A dynamic, filterable collection of references.
  • Open Science Commitment: Aligns with the principles of open science.
Key Publications:
  • Suber, P. (2012). Open Access
  • Karaganis, J. (Ed.). (2018). Shadow Libraries

Democratic Knowledge

Dream: A world where knowledge empowers democracy, fostering critical thinking, civic engagement, and social justice.

How MLKN.lab Contributes:
  • RFCDC Integration: Uses the Reference Framework of Competences for Democratic Culture.
  • Education Focus: Aligns with democratic values.
  • Cognitive Interfaces: Designs tools that empower users.
Key Publications:
  • Council of Europe. (2018). Reference Framework of Competences for Democratic Culture
  • Van Noorden, R. (2015). Interdisciplinary Research by the Numbers

A Sustainable Future

Dream: A world where knowledge helps address global challenges, such as climate change, inequality, and geopolitical crises.

How MLKN.lab Contributes:
  • Systems Science Focus: Models knowledge as a complex system.
  • Interdisciplinary Connections: Links environmental science, AI, and policy.
  • Societal Impact: Designs tools that address real-world problems.
Key Publications:
  • Bertalanffy, L. von. (1968). General System Theory
  • UNESCO. (2021). Recommendation on Open Science
